2. The Mathematical Formulas for Inventory Optimization

Our laboratory utilizes the verified algebraic frameworks established by F.W. Harris to ensure 100% parity with international logistics standards:

Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) Formula

EOQ = √ [ (2 × Demand × Order Cost) / Holding Cost ]

Reorder Point (ROP) Formula

ROP = (Daily Usage × Lead Time) + Safety Stock
  • Annual Demand: The total sum of units expected to be sold in a fiscal year.
  • Carrying Cost: The total capital expenditure required to house one unit of stock (warehousing, insurance, tax).
  • Lead Time: The temporal horizon between placing an order and physical receipt of goods.
  • Setup Cost: The labor and administrative cost of initiating a purchase order.

5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the "Holding Cost" comprised of?

In institutional finance, holding cost includes warehouse rent, utilities, insurance, labor, and the "Opportunity Cost" of the capital tied up in stock.

Is the EOQ model accurate for all businesses?

The EOQ model is highly reliable for businesses with steady demand. For highly seasonal or volatile industries, we recommend auditing the **Safety Stock** variables within our matrix.
