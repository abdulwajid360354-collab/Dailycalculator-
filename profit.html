2. The Mathematical Formulas for Profit & Margin Analytics

Our laboratory utilizes the verified algebraic frameworks established by global financial institutions to ensure 100% parity with professional reporting:

Gross Profit Formula

Gross Profit = Revenue - COGS

Net Profit Formula (Post-Operating Expenses)

Net Profit = Revenue - (COGS + OpEx)

Profit Margin vs. Markup Logic

Margin (%) = (Net Profit / Revenue) × 100
Markup (%) = (Net Profit / Cost) × 100

5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the difference between Margin and Markup?

Margin is profit relative to the selling price, while Markup is profit relative to the cost. Profit margin can never exceed 100%, but markup can be unlimited.

Why is Gross Profit different from Net Profit?

Gross profit only subtracts the direct cost of the product. Net profit subtracts all additional costs like taxes, rent, and labor, providing the "real" money left over.
