2. The Mathematical Formula for Margin & Markup

Our laboratory utilizes the verified algebraic frameworks used by global consulting firms to ensure 100% parity with professional business reporting:

The Margin Formula (Revenue-Based)

Margin (%) = ((Revenue - Cost) / Revenue) × 100

The Markup Formula (Cost-Based)

Markup (%) = ((Revenue - Cost) / Cost) × 100
  • Cost: The total capital expenditure required to produce or acquire the asset.
  • Revenue: The net liquidity received from the transaction.
  • Gross Profit: The absolute dollar difference between Revenue and Cost.

5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the difference between Margin and Markup?

Margin is profit relative to the selling price, while Markup is profit relative to the cost price. Margin can never exceed 100%, but Markup can be unlimited.

Is Gross Margin the same as Net Margin?

No. Gross margin only accounts for COGS. Net margin accounts for all expenses, including taxes, rent, and administrative costs. This tool provides the foundational Gross Margin audit.
