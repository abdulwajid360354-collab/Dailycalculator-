2. The Mathematical Formulas for Asset Amortization

Our laboratory utilizes the verified algebraic frameworks established by global accounting institutions to ensure 100% parity with international standards:

A. Straight Line Depreciation (Standard)

Annual Expense = (Cost - Salvage Value) / Useful Life

B. Double Declining Balance (Accelerated)

Expense = 2 × Straight Line Rate × Beginning Book Value

C. Sum-of-the-Years' Digits (SYD)

Expense = (Remaining Life / Sum of Years) × (Cost - Salvage)
  • Historical Cost: The original purchase price plus taxes and setup costs.
  • Salvage Value: The estimated residual liquidity of the asset at the end of its life.
  • Useful Life: The temporal horizon during which the asset provides economic utility.
  • Accumulated Depreciation: The total sum of depreciation expensed to date.

5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why does salvage value stop depreciation?

In professional accounting, an asset cannot be depreciated below its estimated salvage value. Our tool automatically "caps" the final year's expense to ensure compliance with this rule.

What is the "Book Value"?

Book value is the original cost of the asset minus the total accumulated depreciation. It represents the value of the asset as recorded on the balance sheet.
