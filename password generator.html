2. The Mathematical Logic of Password Entropy

Our laboratory utilizes the verified algebraic frameworks used by global cryptographers to ensure 100% parity with international security standards. The logic is predicated on the measurement of bits of entropy:

Entropy (E) = log2(RL) = L × log2(R)
  • L: The absolute length of the password string.
  • R: The pool of available characters (Cardinality).
  • CSPRNG: Cryptographically Secure Pseudo-Random Number Generator, ensuring non-predictability at the silicon level.
  • Brute-Force Threshold: The theoretical time required for a supercomputer to exhaust the search space ($2^E$ combinations).

What is CSPRNG?

Unlike standard random generators used in games, a Cryptographically Secure Pseudo-Random Number Generator uses external entropy (like CPU thermals) to ensure the numbers cannot be predicted or reversed by hackers.
