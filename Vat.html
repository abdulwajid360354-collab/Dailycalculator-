2. The Mathematical Formulas for VAT Modeling

Our laboratory utilizes the verified algebraic frameworks used by global tax authorities to ensure 100% parity with professional reporting. The logic varies based on the audit direction:

Method A: Calculating Total Including VAT (Add VAT)

Total Amount = Net Amount × (1 + (VAT Rate / 100))

Method B: Reversing VAT (Remove VAT)

Net Amount = Gross Total / (1 + (VAT Rate / 100))
  • Gross Price: The final consumer price inclusive of all government levies.
  • Net Price: The actual asset value before taxation is applied.
  • VAT Component: The absolute difference between Gross and Net (The government's share).

5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How does VAT differ from Sales Tax?

VAT is a consumption tax placed on a product whenever value is added at each stage of the supply chain. Sales Tax is typically a one-time tax collected at the final point of sale to the end-user.
