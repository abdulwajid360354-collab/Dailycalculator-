2. The Mathematical Formula for Net Cash Flow

Our laboratory utilizes the verified algebraic frameworks establish by international accounting bodies to ensure 100% parity with professional financial reporting. The logic is predicated on the Direct Method of cash accounting:

Net Cash Flow = (COperating + CInvesting + CFinancing)
  • Operating Cash Flow (OCF): Cash generated from core business production.
  • Investing Cash Flow (ICF): Cash used for or generated from asset acquisition/disposal.
  • Financing Cash Flow (FCF): Cash movement from debt issuance, repayments, or equity dividends.
  • Net Position: The actual surplus or deficit of liquid capital after all flows are reconciled.

5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is Cash Flow the same as Profit?

No. Profit (Accrual) measures revenue minus expenses, regardless of when cash is received. Cash Flow tracks the actual movement of liquidity. A profitable company can still go bankrupt if its cash flow is negative.

What is Free Cash Flow (FCF)?

FCF is the cash remaining after a company pays for its operating expenses and capital expenditures. It is the primary metric investors use to determine a company's ability to pay dividends.
