2. The Mathematical Formulas for RAID Capacity

Our laboratory utilizes the verified algebraic frameworks used by global hardware manufacturers to ensure 100% parity with institutional standards:

RAID 5 (Parity Standard)

Usable Capacity = (n - 1) × C

RAID 6 (Double Parity Standard)

Usable Capacity = (n - 2) × C

RAID 10 (Striped Mirror)

Usable Capacity = (n / 2) × C
  • n: Number of physical disks in the array.
  • C: Capacity of the smallest individual disk.
  • Fault Tolerance: The number of simultaneous drive failures the array can survive without total data loss.
  • Write Overhead: The computational penalty incurred by parity calculation (highest in RAID 6).

5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the safest RAID level?

For institutional safety, RAID 6 is considered superior as it can survive two simultaneous disk failures. However, RAID 10 is preferred for performance-critical environments like SQL databases.

Why is my usable space lower than advertised?

In RAID configurations, space is sacrificed to store "Parity" or "Mirrors." This is the insurance policy for your data; without this overhead, a single disk failure would result in absolute data loss.
